Referral network

Hotel concierge, casino security, property managers. Non-fee relationship only. High-value premises liability cases unique to the Las Vegas market.
Labor & union channel
Culinary Workers Union Local 226 (~60k members), Ironworkers, Laborers, Carpenters. Workers' comp + third-party PI. Spanish-language capability essential.
Past client re-referral
90-day, 6-month, and 12-month post-close outreach. Past clients convert at 3–5× the rate of cold leads. Lowest possible acquisition cost.

Case sign-up
Consultation · conflict check · NV RPC 1.5(c) fee agreement
▾
⚠NV RPC 1.5(c): All contingency fee agreements must be in writing and signed by the client. A verbal agreement is unenforceable. No work begins without an executed fee agreement in the file.
StageFee
Pre-suit / pre-answer33.3% of gross recovery
Post-suit / post-answer40% of gross recovery
Post-verdict / arbitration award45% of gross recovery
Case costsAdvanced by firm; deducted from recovery — must specify pre- or post-fee in the agreement
Conflict check
Run in Filevine before signing. Check opposing parties, insurers, referring attorneys. NV RPC 1.7 — no adverse interests without disclosure.
Medical authorizations
Execute HIPAA authorizations at signing — one per treating provider. Delays here delay the entire case. Send retrieval requests within 72 hours.
Referral fee consent
If a referral fee is payable, client must provide informed written consent to the division and each attorney's share — NV RPC 1.5(e).

    Pillar 4

    Medical & Treatment Relationships

    LOP network · expert roster · treatment gaps · IME defense
    ℹIn PI litigation, medical records and treatment are the damages. A client who treats consistently and has well-documented injuries will always produce a better outcome. Cavanaugh Law's role is to ensure appropriate care is received and the medical narrative supports the legal case.
    LOP (Letter of Protection) provider network
    Provider typeRole & strategy
    ChiropractorsHighest-volume LOP providers for MVA. Treat soft-tissue injuries, document functional limitations. Build relationships along I-15 and US-95 corridors.
    Orthopedic surgeonsNecessary for fractures, joint injuries, and surgical cases — higher case value when surgery is required. Las Vegas has a strong supply of orthopedic practices.
    Pain management / interventionalInjections, nerve blocks, spinal procedures. Document chronic pain and future medical needs. Key for high-value demand letters.
    Imaging centers (MRI)Early MRI documentation is critical. Establish preferred imaging partners who produce clear reports and treat on LOP.
    Physical therapy & rehabDocument functional impairment and recovery progress. Consistent PT records support non-economic damages narrative.
    Urgent care & ERsFirst-day treatment is the most important record in the file. Build relationships with urgent care providers near high-accident corridors.
    ⚠LOP relationships are non-fee only — NV RPC 5.4. No per-referral compensation to providers. Value exchange: co-branded patient education, office visits, appreciation events only. Negotiate lien reduction agreements with LOP providers in advance — not after settlement.
    Expert witness roster
    Accident reconstructionists
    For contested liability: trucking, multi-vehicle, pedestrian, bicycle. Build relationships with 2–3 certified reconstructionists before you need them.
    Life care planners
    Future medical cost projections for catastrophic injury, TBI, and spinal cord cases. Essential for seven-figure demands.
    Vocational rehabilitation
    Lost earning capacity for clients with career-impacting injuries. Pair with economist for present value calculations.
    Neuropsychologists
    TBI and cognitive impairment cases — testing, functional assessment, and expert testimony on cognitive limitations.
    Biomechanical engineers
    To establish mechanism of injury, especially for low-impact MVA cases where the defense argues forces were insufficient to cause injury.
    Economists
    Present value of future damages, lost wages, and household services. Required for any case with significant ongoing economic loss.
    Treatment gap management & IME defense

    Treatment continuity & IME prep
    Gap prevention · IME defense strategy
    ▾
    Treatment gap prevention
    Monthly client check-in calls to confirm treatment status. If client stops treating, document the reason. Address transportation barriers early. Advise clients in writing that gaps affect case value.
    IME defense
    Prepare client for IME in advance — be honest, describe all symptoms, don't minimize. Send a representative to observe where Nevada law permits. Obtain a rebuttal report from treating physician after IME report is received.

    Ethics, Malpractice & Compliance

    Nevada RPC · advertising rules · referral fee compliance · trust accounting · malpractice avoidance
    Nevada RPC — key rules for PI practice
    RulePI firm application
    NV RPC 1.5(a)Fees must be reasonable. Factors: time and labor, difficulty, skill required, customary fee, results obtained.
    NV RPC 1.5(c)Contingency fee agreements must be in writing, signed by the client, stating the specific percentage at each stage.
    NV RPC 1.5(e)Fee-splitting between attorneys: proportionate to work done or joint responsibility assumed; client must consent in writing.
    NV RPC 1.7Conflict of interest. Conflicts check at intake on all parties, insurers, and referring attorneys before signing any case.
    NV RPC 1.15Safekeeping client property. IOLTA trust account. No commingling. Prompt disbursement at settlement.
    NV RPC 5.4No sharing of legal fees with non-attorneys. Medical providers, body shops, and referral services cannot receive referral fees.
    NV RPC 7.1Communications must not be false or misleading. Case results must include appropriate disclaimers.
    NV RPC 7.2Advertising. May pay for advertising; may not pay for referrals from non-attorneys. Testimonials require specific disclosures.
    NV RPC 7.3Solicitation. Real-time solicitation prohibited. Targeted direct mail regulated and requires specific disclosures.
    Advertising compliance — Nevada specifics
    ⚠Nevada's attorney advertising rules are stricter than most states. The State Bar reviews advertising and has issued guidance on testimonials, case results, and specialty claims. Submit for an advisory opinion before launching any new campaign if uncertain.
    Required disclosures
    Firm name and contact on all advertising. Case results: past results do not guarantee future outcomes. Testimonials: may not be representative of all clients' experiences.
    Prohibited claims
    "Best", "top", "expert", "specialist" only if certified by an approved organization. No language implying guaranteed results. Spanish-language advertising must comply with the same rules as English.
    Trust account compliance — IOLTA
    RequirementProcedure
    SegregationSeparate IOLTA account for all client funds — never in the operating account. Zero commingling tolerated.
    Client ledgerAccurate, current separate ledger entry for every client with funds on deposit
    Three-way reconciliationMonthly: bank statement vs. client ledger vs. trust ledger — all three must agree
    No early disbursementNever disburse against uncollected funds. Settlement check must clear before any distribution.
    Lien confirmationAll lien payoffs confirmed before distributing client proceeds. Attorney reviews disbursement statement.
    Record retentionTrust account records retained for 5 years minimum — NV RPC 1.15
    Malpractice avoidance
    Malpractice typePrevention
    Missed SOL Most commonSOL in Filevine at intake. Automated reminders at 12mo, 6mo, 3mo, 60 days, 30 days. File suit when SOL <90 days regardless of negotiation status.
    Failure to communicateMonthly written case status updates. Return client calls within 24 business hours. Document every significant communication.
    Inadequate investigationInvestigative checklist at intake: police report, witness statements, surveillance footage (time-sensitive). Investigative checklist must be complete before Stage 2 closes.
    Settlement without authorityNever settle without explicit written client authority. Document every settlement discussion and authorization.
    Conflict of interestConflicts check at intake on all parties. Never take adverse positions against former clients without full written disclosure.
